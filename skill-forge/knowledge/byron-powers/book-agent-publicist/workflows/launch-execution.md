# Launch Execution

## Overview

Launch week is the culmination of months of planning. It's when all your publicity efforts converge to create maximum visibility and momentum. This phase covers executing the launch with events, retail partnerships, online campaigns, and the coordinated push that gives your book its best chance at a strong start.

## Workflow: Executing the Launch

### Step 1: The Launch Week Calendar

Build a day-by-day plan for launch week (publication day plus the surrounding days):

```markdown
# Launch Week Calendar: {Book Title}

Publication Date: {date} ({day of week})

## Week Before
- Monday: Final social media teasers, newsletter preview
- Tuesday: Send reminder to personal network
- Wednesday: Confirm all event logistics
- Thursday: Pre-order push on social media
- Friday: Share early reviews and blurbs

## Launch Week
- Monday: {Activity — e.g., newsletter announcement, morning social post}
- Tuesday (Pub Day): {Full launch sequence — see Step 2}
- Wednesday: {Follow-up activity — e.g., podcast episode drops, thank-you posts}
- Thursday: {Activity — e.g., bookstore event, Instagram Live}
- Friday: {Activity — e.g., newsletter to new subscribers, weekend reading push}
- Weekend: {Activity — e.g., book signing, social media engagement}

## Week After
- Continue media appearances
- Share reader reactions and reviews
- Thank everyone who helped
- Assess initial results and adjust ongoing strategy
```

### Step 2: Publication Day Sequence

Publication day should feel like an event. Coordinate timing across channels:

**Morning:**
- Social media announcement (all platforms, staggered by 30-60 minutes)
- Newsletter to your full list
- Personal messages to your closest supporters asking them to share
- Update your website homepage

**Midday:**
- Share purchase links across all retailers (don't favor one — readers have preferences)
- Post behind-the-scenes content (the journey to publication, writing process photos)
- Engage with every comment, share, and mention

**Afternoon/Evening:**
- Launch event (virtual or in-person — see Step 3)
- Share photos and highlights from the event
- Thank your agent, editor, and publisher publicly
- Repost any media coverage that drops on pub day

### Step 3: Launch Events

**In-person launch event:**

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Venue | Bookstore (ideal — they sell books on-site), library, bar, community space |
| Format | Reading (10-15 min) + Q&A (15-20 min) + signing |
| Timing | Evening (7-8 PM start) or weekend afternoon |
| Promotion | Social media, newsletter, local media, bookstore's channels, flyers |
| Logistics | Microphone, podium, signing table, pens, bookmarks, water |
| Attendance goal | 30-50 people is a strong turnout for most authors |

**Virtual launch event:**

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Platform | Zoom, Instagram Live, YouTube Live, or publisher's platform |
| Format | Reading + Q&A + conversation with another author or moderator |
| Timing | Evening (7-8 PM in your primary time zone) |
| Promotion | Same as in-person, plus event registration link |
| Logistics | Good lighting, stable internet, quiet background, tested tech |
| Attendance goal | 50-100 attendees is strong for virtual |

**Hybrid approach:** Host an in-person event and livestream it. Best of both worlds, but requires more technical setup.

**Launch event tips:**
- Partner with a local bookstore — they handle book sales and often help promote
- Invite a fellow author to moderate or do a joint event (doubles the audience)
- Keep the reading short (10-15 minutes max) — audiences prefer conversation
- Have a sign-up sheet for your newsletter
- Bring bookmarks or postcards as takeaways
- Record the event for social media content later

### Step 4: Retail Strategy

**Independent bookstores:**
- Contact local independents 2-3 months before publication
- Offer to do events, signings, or window displays
- Ask if they'll hand-sell your book (personal recommendation to customers)
- Provide signed bookplates for stock copies
- Build relationships — indie booksellers are powerful advocates

**Online retailers:**
- Ensure your book's metadata is correct on all platforms (description, categories, keywords)
- Encourage reviews on Amazon and Goodreads starting on pub day
- If eligible, apply for BookBub featured deals (post-launch, for discounted promotions)
- Monitor your Amazon categories — being in the right category affects discoverability

**Library outreach:**
- Contact your local library system about stocking the book
- Offer to do library events (readings, book clubs, author talks)
- Libraries drive discovery — readers who borrow often become buyers for future books

### Step 5: Social Media Launch Campaign

**Content calendar for launch week:**

| Day | Content Type | Platform Focus |
|-----|-------------|----------------|
| Day -3 | Countdown + cover reveal recap | Instagram, Twitter/X |
| Day -2 | Behind-the-scenes writing journey | Instagram Stories, TikTok |
| Day -1 | "Tomorrow" teaser + pre-order reminder | All platforms |
| Pub Day | Launch announcement + purchase links | All platforms, newsletter |
| Day +1 | Share first reviews and reader reactions | Twitter/X, Instagram |
| Day +2 | Reading or excerpt video | TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube |
| Day +3 | Thank-you post + event highlights | All platforms |

**Social media rules for launch week:**
- Post daily (at minimum) on your primary platforms
- Respond to every comment and mention
- Share (with permission) reader photos, reviews, and reactions
- Don't just post "buy my book" — share stories, behind-the-scenes content, and genuine enthusiasm
- Tag your publisher, agent, and bookstores in relevant posts
- Use your book's hashtag consistently

### Step 6: Mobilize Your Network

**Your "street team" — people who actively help promote:**
- Close friends and family (ask them to post on pub day, buy copies, leave reviews)
- Fellow authors (cross-promotion, social media shares, newsletter mentions)
- Professional contacts (LinkedIn shares, workplace book clubs)
- Online community members (writing groups, genre communities)
- Early readers and ARC reviewers

**How to ask for help without being annoying:**
- Be specific: "Would you be willing to post about my book on Instagram on Tuesday?" is better than "Help me spread the word"
- Make it easy: Provide sample social media copy, images, and links they can use
- Be grateful: Thank everyone publicly and privately
- Don't over-ask: One request per person per launch. Respect their generosity.

## Techniques

### The Momentum Stacking Method
Schedule media appearances, social media posts, and events to overlap and reinforce each other. A podcast episode dropping the same day as a bookstore event creates more buzz than either alone.

### The Gratitude Loop Method
Every time someone helps promote your book, thank them publicly (tag them, share their post, mention them in your newsletter). This encourages more sharing and builds goodwill for future books.

### The Content Repurposing Method
One interview can become: a social media quote graphic, a newsletter excerpt, a blog post, a TikTok clip, and a website testimonial. Maximize every piece of content you create or receive during launch.

### The Week Two Push Method
Many authors exhaust their energy on launch week and go silent. Keep pushing in week two — share new reviews, post reader reactions, do a "one week later" reflection post. Sustained visibility matters more than a single spike.

## Deliverables

By the end of this phase, you should have:
- A detailed launch week calendar with daily activities
- Launch event(s) planned and confirmed
- Social media content calendar and assets prepared
- Street team briefed and ready
- Retail partnerships established
- Tracking system in place for launch metrics
- A post-launch continuation plan
