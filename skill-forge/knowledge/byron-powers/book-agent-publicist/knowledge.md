# Book Agent & Publicist

## Overview

Book Agent & Publicist covers everything between finishing a manuscript and building a sustainable author career. It's the business companion to the Novelist and Technical Author powers — picking up where craft leaves off and handling the commercial, strategic, and public-facing side of publishing.

Whether you're querying your first novel, negotiating a multi-book deal, planning a publicity campaign, or building a long-term author brand, this power meets you where you are.

## Getting Started

When a user activates this power, begin by asking which track fits their current needs:

**Ask the user:** "Where are you in the publishing process?"

**Option 1: Representation Track** — "I need an agent. I want help finding, querying, and landing literary representation."

**Option 2: Deal & Contract Track** — "I have agent interest or a deal in progress. I need help with negotiations, contracts, and publisher relationships."

**Option 3: Publicity Track** — "I have a book coming out (or already out). I need help with publicity, media, and launch strategy."

**Option 4: Career Track** — "I'm thinking long-term. I need help with author branding, platform building, and career strategy."

**Option 5: Full Journey** — "Walk me through everything from finding an agent to building a career."

Based on their answer, guide them into the appropriate track below. Users can switch tracks or access any phase at any time — the tracks are starting points, not constraints.

## Representation Track

The Representation Track covers finding, evaluating, and securing literary representation. It's organized into three phases:

### Phases

1. **Agent Research** — Identify the right agents for your book and build a strategic query list
2. **Query Campaign** — Execute a disciplined, batch-based querying strategy with tracking and iteration
3. **Agent Evaluation** — Evaluate offers of representation, conduct "the call," and choose the right partner

### Representation Track Steering Files

- **agent-research** — Research literary agents systematically using deal databases, wish lists, interviews, and genre fit analysis to build a ranked query list
- **query-campaign** — Run a structured query campaign with batch strategy, response tracking, revision triggers, and nudge protocols
- **agent-evaluation** — Evaluate offers of representation, prepare for "the call," ask the right questions, and choose the agent who fits your career

## Deal & Contract Track

The Deal & Contract Track covers what happens after you have representation — from submission to publishers through contract negotiation. It's organized into three phases:

### Phases

4. **Submission to Publishers** — Understand the editor submission process and how your agent shops your book
5. **Deal Negotiation** — Navigate offers, auctions, advances, and the key terms that matter
6. **Contract Literacy** — Understand publishing contracts, rights, royalties, and the clauses that protect you

### Deal & Contract Track Steering Files

- **submission-to-publishers** — Understand how agents submit to editors, the editorial acquisition process, submission rounds, and how to handle rejections and revisions at the publisher level
- **deal-negotiation** — Navigate book deal structures including advances, royalties, auctions, pre-empts, multi-book deals, and the financial realities of traditional publishing
- **contract-literacy** — Understand publishing contract terms, rights grants, reversion clauses, option clauses, non-compete provisions, and what to negotiate

## Publicity Track

The Publicity Track covers getting your book in front of readers through media, events, and strategic outreach. It's organized into three phases:

### Phases

7. **Publicity Planning** — Build a publicity timeline and strategy tailored to your book and genre
8. **Media & Press** — Pitch media outlets, prepare for interviews, and generate press coverage
9. **Launch Execution** — Execute the book launch with events, partnerships, and coordinated promotion

### Publicity Track Steering Files

- **publicity-planning** — Build a publicity strategy and timeline covering pre-launch, launch week, and post-launch phases with channel selection and audience targeting
- **media-and-press** — Pitch journalists, podcasters, and reviewers with tailored angles, prepare for interviews, and build a press kit
- **launch-execution** — Execute a book launch with event planning, retail partnerships, online campaigns, street teams, and first-week momentum tactics

## Career Track

The Career Track covers the long game — building a sustainable author career beyond any single book. It's organized into three phases:

### Phases

10. **Author Platform** — Build and maintain your public presence across channels that matter for your genre
11. **Brand & Identity** — Define your author brand, visual identity, and consistent public persona
12. **Career Strategy** — Plan multi-book arcs, income diversification, and long-term career sustainability

### Career Track Steering Files

- **author-platform** — Build and grow your author platform across social media, newsletters, speaking, and community engagement with genre-appropriate channel strategy
- **brand-and-identity** — Define your author brand including visual identity, messaging, tone, website, and consistent public persona across all touchpoints
- **career-strategy** — Plan long-term author career strategy including multi-book planning, income diversification, rights management, and sustainable creative output

## Tips for Using This Power

- Activate the steering file for whichever phase you're working on
- Keep a running document for your publishing project (agent list, submission tracker, publicity plan, brand guide)
- Reference earlier phase outputs as you move forward — consistency matters
- Don't try to perfect one phase before moving on; iteration is part of the process
- When stuck, try jumping to a different phase for fresh perspective
- You can switch between tracks at any time — the boundaries are guides, not walls
- The publishing landscape changes constantly — verify specific agent/publisher details before acting on them
- This power complements the Novelist and Technical Author powers — use them together for the complete journey from manuscript to career
